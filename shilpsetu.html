Our Origin

What is ShilpSetu?

ShilpSetu — meaning "Bridge of Crafts" in Sanskrit — is a platform born from a deep reverence for India's extraordinary artisan heritage. Across villages and centuries, skilled hands have shaped clay, woven silk, carved wood, and painted stories onto fabric and stone.

"Every thread tells a story. Every purchase keeps a tradition alive."

Yet today, many of these traditions stand on the edge of being forgotten. Urbanisation, changing tastes, and economic pressures have pushed thousands of craft families out of their ancestral vocations. India risks losing not just objects, but entire living languages of form and meaning.

We bridge that gap — connecting master craftspeople from remote corners of India with a world that is ready to appreciate, celebrate, and preserve their work. When you discover a piece on ShilpSetu, you are holding centuries of memory in your hands.

India is not merely a nation or a territory. It is a civilisation — and its crafts are the most honest expression of that civilisation's soul.

— The ShilpSetu Vision

Crafts We Celebrate

From the blue pottery of Jaipur to the Kantha embroidery of Bengal, ShilpSetu is home to hundreds of distinct craft traditions — each a universe unto itself.

🪴
Blue Pottery
Jaipur, Rajasthan

A Central Asian art form refined in Mughal courts and now synonymous with Jaipur. Fired with quartz paste, no clay — each piece is unique and translucent.

Ceramic

Banarasi Weaving
Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh

Silk sarees threaded with real gold and silver — a tradition spanning 2,000 years and the preferred choice for royal weddings across the subcontinent.

Rosewood Carving
Saharanpur, UP

Intricate floral latticework carved into rosewood and sheesham — a craft passed down through Muslim artisan families for over five centuries.

Madhubani Painting
Mithila, Bihar

Ritual folk paintings made by women to decorate walls and floors during festivals — now a globally celebrated art form with bold geometric storytelling.

Looking Forward

Our Vision & Impact

Our Mission

Why We Exist

ShilpSetu exists to ensure that India's extraordinary craft traditions do not become museum exhibits or historical footnotes — but remain living, breathing, economically vibrant expressions of a civilisation's genius.

  • Provide artisans with fair, direct access to national and global markets
  • Document and digitally archive endangered craft techniques
  • Create educational resources for schools and craft enthusiasts
  • Advocate for GI (Geographical Indication) protections for craft communities
  • Build mentorship pipelines from master artisans to young apprentices

The Problem We Solve

Why It Matters Now

India's handloom and handicraft sector supports over 200 million livelihoods — making it the second-largest rural employer after agriculture. Yet the sector is in crisis:

  • Thousands of unique craft forms have already been lost in a single generation
  • Master artisans earn less than unskilled daily wage workers in many regions
  • Children of craftspeople actively abandon the tradition due to economic insecurity
  • Cheap imitation goods undercut authentic handmade pieces in local markets
  • Global buyers lack a trusted platform to discover and purchase authentic Indian crafts
