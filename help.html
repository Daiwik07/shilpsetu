Frequently Asked Questions

Indian artisans are skilled craftspeople who create handmade products using traditional techniques passed down through generations. From weaving silk and carving wood to shaping clay and painting pottery, they preserve India’s cultural identity through their craftsmanship.

Indian handicrafts date back thousands of years, even to the time of the Indus Valley Civilization. Every region of India developed its own unique art forms, materials, and styles. These crafts were not just products — they were symbols of culture, community, and tradition.

Despite their talent, many artisans struggle with low income, lack of recognition, and competition from machine-made goods. Middlemen often take most of the profits, and limited access to digital platforms makes it difficult for them to reach modern customers. Because of this, many young people are leaving traditional crafts.

Supporting artisans means preserving history, promoting sustainable handmade products, and empowering hardworking families. Handmade crafts are eco-friendly, unique, and carry a human touch that machines cannot replicate.

ShilpSetu is a digital bridge that connects traditional Indian artisans with the modern generation. It aims to showcase their work, share their stories, and create awareness about their challenges — ensuring that India’s artistic heritage continues to thrive in the digital age.

You can support artisans by choosing handmade products, spreading awareness, and valuing traditional craftsmanship. Even a small action can help keep centuries-old art forms alive.
