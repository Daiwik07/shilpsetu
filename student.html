Our Mission

WHY WE
BUILT THIS

We are students who believe that education doesn't end in the classroom. India has 7 million living artisans — each one carrying techniques older than most nations. We built this project to make those techniques visible, understandable, and valued by the next generation of leaders, designers, and citizens.

From researching ancient Shilpa Shastras to mapping the Pehchaan card welfare system, every section of this project is a piece of genuine curiosity, put into honest work.

What We Stand For

OUR VALUES

🔍
Honest Research
We went to primary sources — government portals, artisan testimonials, policy documents. No copy-paste, no shortcuts. Every data point in this project was verified.
🤝
Respect for the Artisan
The artisan is not a subject of study — they are the teacher. Every page of this project is built around what they do, not just what we think about what they do.
💡
Student Perspective
We are not experts. We are curious. This project is our genuine attempt to understand something larger than ourselves — and to share what we found with our peers.
🌱
Long-term Thinking
This is not a one-time assignment. The conversations this project starts — about craft, culture, and conscious consumption — are ones we intend to carry far beyond this semester.
